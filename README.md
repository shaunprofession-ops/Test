Slide 2 — How We Test
We check our systems from 4 different angles. One tester pretends to be a complete stranger trying to break in from the internet. Another logs in like a regular user and tries to misuse the access. The other two are automated scans — one from outside, one from inside our network with login access.
Bottom line: No single test catches everything, so we run all four. If one misses something, another usually picks it up.
Slide 3 — Two Types of Pentesting
A side-by-side of Black Box vs Grey Box testing. Black Box is the "outsider with no info" view. Grey Box is the "what if a logged-in user goes rogue, or their account gets stolen" view.
Bottom line: We cover both the threat from outside and the threat from someone already inside. One without the other leaves a blind spot.
Slide 4 — The Numbers
Quick scoreboard of all issues found so far across our servers and our applications. Shows how many are closed, how many are still open, and how serious each one is (Critical, High, Medium, Low).
Bottom line: This is the snapshot of where we stand. The Open Critical and High numbers are the ones the board should keep an eye on.
Slide 5 — Application Vulnerabilities
We've already fixed 64% of the issues found in our applications. The remaining 36% are listed app-by-app — what's pending, why it's taking time (some need a version upgrade, some are vendor limitations, some need the app to be redesigned), and the cost and timeline to close them.
Bottom line: A few apps need real investment to fully secure — MAGIC needs ~1.25 Cr, Emarine ~25 lacs, and a few others around 10 lacs each. Budget calls sit here.
Slide 6 — Ageing of Open Issues
Shows how long our open issues have been sitting unresolved over time. Lower percentages mean we're closing them faster than they're piling up.
Bottom line: Tells us if we're actually keeping pace or falling behind. Worth flagging if Critical or High ageing keeps climbing month over month.
